# Idea - Automotive Industry in Indonesia

## The Idea
> Indonesia is Southeast Asia's largest automotive market, with over 1 million cars and 6 million motorcycles sold annually. The industry is undergoing a fundamental shift — from combustion engines to electric vehicles — driven by government policy, rising fuel costs, and global manufacturer investment.

## Market Overview

- **Indonesia rank:** #1 in ASEAN for automotive sales volume
- **Dominant segment:** motorcycles (6x more units sold than cars)
- **Car market leaders:** Toyota, Honda, Daihatsu, Suzuki, Mitsubishi
- **Motorcycle market leaders:** Honda (70%+ share), Yamaha, Kawasaki
- **Production hub:** Indonesia exports vehicles to 80+ countries — not just a consumer market

### Indonesian Conglomerates
- **Astra International** — largest automotive group, distributes Toyota, Honda, Daihatsu, BMW
- **Indomobil** — distributes Nissan, Suzuki, Volkswagen, Hino

## Electric Vehicle (EV) Transition

Indonesia is betting big on EVs, driven by two factors:
1. **Nickel reserves** — Indonesia holds 40%+ of global nickel supply, a key EV battery material
2. **Government targets** — 20% of vehicles sold to be electric by 2030

### Current EV Landscape
- **Most affordable:** Wuling Air EV (~Rp 200-250 juta)
- **Most popular:** Hyundai Ioniq 5, Ioniq 6
- **Motorcycle EVs:** Gesits, Volta, Alva (ALVA One) — homegrown Indonesian brands
- **Infrastructure gap:** charging stations still sparse outside Java

### Policy Drivers
- Import tax exemptions for EV manufacturers who commit to local production
- Luxury tax (PPnBM) waived for EVs
- Government fleet electrification mandate

## Digital Disruption in Automotive

- **Online car buying:** OLX Autos, Carro, Moladin disrupting used car market
- **Ride-hailing impact:** Gojek and Grab reducing private car ownership intent among urban youth
- **Subscription models:** emerging — pay monthly for a car without ownership
- **Telematics & IoT:** fleet tracking, predictive maintenance entering the mainstream
- **Financing tech:** multifinance apps, BNPL for motorcycles (Adira, WOM Finance going digital)

## Challenges

- **Charging infrastructure** — outside Jakarta, charging is unreliable
- **Battery cost** — EVs still 2-3x more expensive than comparable ICE vehicles
- **Consumer trust** — range anxiety, resale value concerns
- **Spare parts ecosystem** — EV mechanics and parts availability still thin outside major cities

## Why It Matters
- Indonesia's automotive market reflects the broader tension between legacy industry and digital/green transition
- The EV push is one of the few sectors where Indonesian-made products (Gesits, Volta) compete with global brands
- Mobility data, fleet management, and vehicle financing are ripe for automation — same patterns as logistics and payments
