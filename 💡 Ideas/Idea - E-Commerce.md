# Idea - E-Commerce

## The Idea
> E-commerce is the buying and selling of goods and services over the internet. What started as simple online storefronts has evolved into a complex ecosystem of logistics, payments, personalization, and automation — now one of the largest sectors in the global economy.

## What Is E-Commerce?

### Core Models
| Model | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| B2C | Business to Consumer | Tokopedia, Shopee |
| B2B | Business to Business | Alibaba, Ralali |
| C2C | Consumer to Consumer | Carousell, eBay |
| D2C | Direct to Consumer | Brand sells directly, no middleman |
| B2G | Business to Government | Procurement platforms |

### How It Works (Simplified)
1. **Storefront** — product catalog, search, recommendations
2. **Cart & Checkout** — payment gateway, promo codes, tax
3. **Order Management** — inventory sync, fulfillment routing
4. **Logistics** — warehouse, last-mile delivery, returns
5. **Post-purchase** — reviews, loyalty, re-engagement

## Key Trends (2024-2026)

- **Social commerce** — buying directly inside Instagram, TikTok, without leaving the app
- **Quick commerce (q-commerce)** — sub-30-minute delivery; Grab, GoTo pushing hard here in SEA
- **AI personalization** — recommendation engines, dynamic pricing, chatbot support
- **Cross-border e-commerce** — SEA sellers reaching global buyers via TikTok Shop, Shopee International
- **Headless commerce** — decoupled frontend/backend for faster, more flexible storefronts
- **Buy Now Pay Later (BNPL)** — Akulaku, Kredivo driving adoption in Indonesia especially among the unbanked

## Southeast Asia Context

- SEA e-commerce projected to reach **$230B by 2026** (Google Temasek Bain report)
- Indonesia is the largest market — driven by mobile-first consumers, COD dominance, and rising middle class
- Key platforms: **Shopee, Tokopedia (now TikTok Shop), Lazada, Blibli**
- Pain points unique to SEA: fragmented logistics, low credit card penetration, cash-on-delivery still significant

## E-Commerce and Automation

Where automation intersects with e-commerce operations:
- **Inventory management** — auto-reorder triggers, stock sync across channels
- **Order processing** — automated fulfillment routing, invoice generation
- **Customer comms** — order status bots, complaint triage
- **Finance ops** — payment reconciliation, seller disbursements, tax reporting
- **Data pipelines** — sales dashboards, return rate tracking, cohort analysis

This is directly relevant to internal tooling work — payment requests, invoicing automation, and data pipelines follow the same patterns as e-commerce back-office systems.
